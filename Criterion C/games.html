1. The Lord of the Flies Conch

Description: The Lord of the Flies is a fairly famous book about a group of British School boys stranded on an island, trying to survive and govern themselves. At one point in the book, the boys pick up a conch and decide that whenever they need to make a decision or declare something, one of them will hold the conch and speak, while the others wait for their chance at the conch


Similarly, when facing troubles in groups, what students can do is, take an object, which can be anything from a water bottle to an eraser, and take turns to speak their point of view, before rationally planning out and deciding the course of action

Objective: Students take turns to speak their point of view and listen to other before collaborating and finding a solution that fits the situation best


Time: Roughly 5-15 mins, each student should get to speak for around 1-2 mins, to save time. It's also important for students to remember to keep it civil and respect each other



2. Freaky Friday

Description: In the unlikely event you haven't watched Freaky Friday I'll briefly explain what it's about. Tt's a movie about a mother and daughter, who are very different people, end up having their bodies switched and they have to live as one another until they find asolution. By being in each others shoe's they were able to develop a new respect andunderstanding of one another. Which brings us to the activity 2 or more parties who are having a fight have to act in the situation as the person they're fighting with.


Objective: The objective of the game is to try and put oneself in another persons shoes and understand their perspective and what they may be feeling, by acting as them in the situation. It's also an opportuniy for one to reflect on their actions and how they impact other people. The main aim to come to a decision that benefits everyone involved


Time Required: Roughly 10 to 30 mins depending on the severity of complexity of the conflict (I recommend keeping it short so you don't waste too much time)



3. Paired Up

Description: One of my main advices while dealing with conflict is to separate the conflicting parties but for this activity, you need to pair them up together. The aim of this activity is to take a rope and tie 2 peoples hands together so that they have one free working hand, but they need to collaborate and function as a unit to be productive.

Now personally I don't recommend this method if you need to be wary of deadlines, but if members have time, and a few members in particular are being annoying, it's a fun way to solve the conflict and force people to work together. You're essentially restricting the problem to the conflicting parties, leaving them to solve it alone while the rest continue with their work


Objective: Forcing 2 conflicting parties to collaborate by tying their hands together, so that they overcome their conflict and work together as one unit.


Time: Really depends on the duration of the task and the attitudes of the members
